# Research Document: How to Evaluate Legal AI Tools: A Framework for Law Firm Decision-Makers

## Brief Summary
Law firms need a structured evaluation framework for selecting legal AI tools that goes beyond feature checklists. The article targets managing partners, legal ops leaders, and IT directors who are past the "should we adopt?" question and need a repeatable, defensible process for comparing vendors. The differentiating angle: adoption rate and workflow integration are the primary evaluation criteria, not afterthoughts.

## Industry Context

The legal AI market crossed an inflection point in 2025-2026. Individual adoption doubled (31% to 69%), but institutional adoption lags badly: only 34% of firms have formally adopted AI, and 43% have no AI policy at all. This gap creates the exact problem our article addresses: firms are spending on AI tools without a structured evaluation process, leading to poor selections and low adoption.

Legal tech spending surged 9.7% in 2025, the fastest growth the industry has ever recorded. Firms are not asking whether to buy AI; they are asking which AI to buy and how to evaluate it. The Thomson Reuters/Georgetown report confirms this shift from experimentation to infrastructure.

The trust gap is the critical issue. Only 22.1% of legal professionals report high trust in gen AI outputs. This means evaluation frameworks that ignore trust-building factors (transparency, accuracy validation, human oversight) will lead to purchases that collect dust. The Wolters Kluwer survey found that 39% cite inadequate training as a barrier and 35% report resistance to change, reinforcing that technology selection without an adoption strategy fails.

## Common Pain Points

1. **Buying tools that don't get used** - Most legal AI tools see under 20% sustained adoption. Firms invest in technology that sits unused because it requires lawyers to change their workflows. Legora solution: integrates directly into Word, iManage, and SharePoint where lawyers already work, achieving 80% active usage at BAHR.

2. **Demo vs. reality gap** - Tools perform impressively on clean demo datasets but underperform on actual firm documents (messy formatting, non-standard clauses, multi-jurisdictional complexity). Legora solution: uses the firm's own precedent library and work product, so outputs reflect the firm's actual standards.

3. **No structured evaluation process** - Firms rely on vendor demos and word-of-mouth rather than systematic criteria. Result: decisions driven by the most persuasive salesperson, not the best fit. Legora solution: transparent architecture that welcomes structured evaluation (adoption metrics, integration depth, security certifications).

4. **Security and compliance anxiety** - 39% cite data privacy as a top concern. Firms worry about client data being used for model training, stored insecurely, or accessible to vendor employees. Legora solution: enterprise-grade security with data isolation, SSO, and audit trails.

5. **Training burden and change fatigue** - 39% say inadequate training is a barrier; 35% report resistance to change. Adding another standalone tool increases the training load. Legora solution: minimal behavior change required because the AI operates inside existing tools (Word, DMS) rather than requiring a new interface.

6. **Lack of ROI measurement** - Firms cannot justify continued spending because they have no baseline metrics for comparison. Legora solution: collaborative workspace design means usage data (how many lawyers, how often, what tasks) is built into the platform.

## Original Analysis

### Synthesized Insight
Combining the Wolters Kluwer finding (firms with visible AI strategy are 4x more likely to see ROI) with the 8am Report data (43% of firms have no AI policy), we can derive: roughly 43% of firms investing in legal AI are doing so without the strategic framework that makes ROI 4x more likely. This is the evaluation gap our article fills. A structured evaluation framework is itself a strategic signal that predicts ROI.

Additionally, combining adoption data (most tools see <20% sustained usage) with Legora's BAHR metrics (80% active, 30% daily power users), the adoption multiplier is 4x the industry norm. This is not a marginal improvement; it suggests a fundamentally different integration approach.

- Derived from: Wolters Kluwer Future Ready Lawyer Survey 2026 + 8am Report 2026 + Legora BAHR adoption data
- Unique takeaway: The single strongest predictor of legal AI ROI is not feature depth or accuracy benchmarks. It is whether the tool integrates into existing workflows tightly enough that lawyers use it daily without conscious effort.

### Content Gap Identified
Existing evaluation frameworks (Ironclad's 4 Cs, Harvey's 7 criteria, Artificial Lawyer's 5 areas) all treat adoption as an outcome to hope for rather than a criterion to evaluate upfront. None of them provide:
1. A weighted scoring methodology where adoption predictors outrank feature lists
2. Specific benchmarks for what "good adoption" looks like (with concrete numbers)
3. Pilot program design guidance (duration, metrics, success criteria)
4. Cost-of-switching analysis alongside cost-of-purchase
5. Change management readiness as a formal evaluation dimension

Our article fills all five gaps.

## Experience Evidence

### Adoption Example
At BAHR, one of the leading Nordic law firms, 80% of lawyers actively use Legora, with 30% engaging 10+ times per day. This is not a pilot metric; it is sustained, firm-wide adoption. For context, the industry average for legal AI tool adoption is under 20%. The 4x difference is attributable to Legora's integration-first approach: the tool operates inside Word and connects to the firm's existing DMS (iManage, SharePoint) rather than requiring lawyers to switch to a separate application.

### Workflow Integration Evidence
Legora's Word Add-in lets lawyers highlight a clause in a contract and immediately get AI-powered suggestions based on the firm's own precedent library. There is no copy-pasting between applications, no context switching, no learning a new interface. The AI reads the document context, understands the clause in relation to the full agreement, and drafts alternatives using language patterns from the firm's previous work. For research, Legora connects to legal databases, DMS, and SharePoint from one interface, reducing the multi-tab, multi-tool research workflow to a single search. Tabular Review handles due diligence at scale: thousands of documents analyzed with AI-suggested markup, replacing manual review cycles that typically take weeks.
