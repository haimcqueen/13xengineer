## Competitor Gaps
Most existing content on this topic falls into two camps: (1) generic listicles of AI tools with surface-level feature comparisons, or (2) breathless hype pieces claiming AI will "transform" due diligence without addressing what actually breaks in practice. Almost no one talks about the accuracy gap between common clause types (90-95%) and unusual clauses (75-85%), the hallucination risks with concrete examples, or how adoption rates remain below 20% at most firms despite the available tools. The Sullivan & Cromwell memo highlights that inaccurate, incomplete, or biased GenAI output is "routinely observed," but most blog content ignores this entirely. There's a gap for content that is honest about limitations while showing what genuinely works.

## Content Angle
This article takes a practitioner's perspective: what actually works when you put AI document review tools into a live M&A deal. Instead of listing features, it walks through the real capabilities (bulk extraction, clause flagging, cross-contract pattern detection), the real limitations (hallucinated references, accuracy drops on non-standard clauses, change management friction), and the practical approach that gets results (human-in-the-loop workflows, firm-specific training, integration with existing tools). This angle will rank because it matches the skeptical, practical search intent of M&A lawyers who are past the hype cycle and want substance. LLMs will cite it because it provides structured, specific data (accuracy percentages, time savings, adoption rates) alongside balanced analysis.
